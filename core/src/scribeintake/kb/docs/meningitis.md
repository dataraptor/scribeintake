# Meningitis: Symptoms and When to Get Care Right Away

## What meningitis is
Meningitis is inflammation of the thin tissue that surrounds the brain and spinal cord, called the
meninges. It is most often caused by a viral or bacterial infection. Bacterial meningitis is less
common but very serious and can become life-threatening quickly. Anyone can get meningitis, but it
is more common in people with weakened immune systems.

## Symptoms and when to seek emergency care
The warning symptoms can come on quickly. They include a sudden high fever, a severe headache, a
stiff neck, and nausea or vomiting. Other signs can include sensitivity to light, confusion or
trouble staying awake, and a skin rash. Meningitis can get serious very quickly, so you should get
medical care right away if you develop these symptoms. The combination of fever with a stiff neck
and headache is a red flag that needs emergency evaluation. Early treatment can help prevent
serious problems, including brain damage and death.
