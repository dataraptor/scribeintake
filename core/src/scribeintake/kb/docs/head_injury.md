# Head Injury and Concussion

## Types of head injury
Head injuries can be open or closed. A closed injury does not break through the skull. With an
open, or penetrating, injury, an object pierces the skull and enters the brain. Common causes
include falls, motor vehicle crashes, sports injuries, and violence. A concussion is a mild brain
injury that can cause headache, confusion, and memory problems. Even a head injury that seems mild
can sometimes lead to bleeding inside the skull, so warning signs should be watched closely in the
hours and days afterward.

## Warning signs that need emergency care
Get medical help right away after a head injury if there is a headache that gets worse or does not
go away, repeated vomiting or nausea, convulsions or seizures, an inability to wake up, slurred
speech, weakness or numbness in the arms or legs, loss of coordination, or increased confusion,
restlessness, or agitation. One pupil larger than the other and clear fluid draining from the nose
or ears are also emergencies. People who take blood thinners (anticoagulants) are at higher risk
of dangerous bleeding after a head injury and should be evaluated promptly even after a seemingly
minor bump to the head. When in doubt, call 911 or go to the emergency room.
