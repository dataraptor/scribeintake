# Abdominal Pain: When to Seek Emergency Care

## Many causes, ranging from mild to serious
Abdominal (belly) pain is very common and has many causes, from indigestion and gas to infections,
appendicitis, gallstones, and bowel problems. Most belly pain is mild and goes away on its own.
Some patterns of pain, however, point to a serious problem that needs urgent care, especially when
the pain is severe, comes on suddenly, or comes with other warning signs.

## Warning signs that need emergency care
Get medical help right away or call 911 if you have sudden, sharp, severe abdominal pain, or if
your belly is rigid, hard, and tender to the touch, which can be a sign of a serious problem inside
the abdomen. Seek emergency care if abdominal pain comes with vomiting blood, blood in the stool
(bright red, maroon, or black and tarry), inability to pass stool along with vomiting, chest or
shoulder pain, or trouble breathing. Severe abdominal pain during pregnancy, or pain with a high
fever, fainting, or signs of dehydration, also needs prompt evaluation.
