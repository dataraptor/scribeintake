# Anaphylaxis: A Life-Threatening Allergic Reaction

## What anaphylaxis is
Anaphylaxis is a serious allergic reaction. It can begin very quickly, and its symptoms may be
life-threatening. The most common causes are reactions to foods (especially peanuts and tree
nuts), medications, and stinging insects. The reaction usually starts within minutes of exposure
to the allergen.

## Symptoms
Symptoms can involve several parts of the body at once. Breathing symptoms include shortness of
breath, coughing, wheezing, and chest tightness. Throat symptoms include itching, tightness,
trouble swallowing, and swelling of the throat or tongue that can block the airway. Skin symptoms
include itching, hives, redness, and swelling. There may also be a weak pulse, dizziness, fainting,
nausea or vomiting, and a feeling of impending doom.

## When to seek emergency care
Anaphylaxis is a medical emergency. If someone is having a serious allergic reaction, call 911
right away. If an epinephrine auto-injector is available, give the injection immediately, then
call 911 — even if the person starts to feel better, because symptoms can return. Swelling of the
throat or tongue, trouble breathing, or fainting after exposure to a known allergen should be
treated as an emergency without delay.
