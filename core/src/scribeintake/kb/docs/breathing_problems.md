# Breathing Problems and Low Blood Oxygen

## What shortness of breath is
When you are short of breath, it is hard or uncomfortable to take in the oxygen your body needs.
You may feel like you cannot get enough air, like you are smothering, or like you have to work
hard to breathe. Many conditions can cause breathing problems, including asthma, emphysema, and
pneumonia, problems with the airway, heart disease, allergies, and anxiety or panic attacks. If
you often have trouble breathing, it is important to find out the cause with a health care
provider.

## Low blood oxygen (hypoxemia)
Hypoxemia means the level of oxygen in your blood is lower than normal. It can happen with severe
breathing problems. Signs that oxygen may be low include severe shortness of breath, fast
breathing, a fast heartbeat, confusion, and a bluish color of the lips, face, or fingernails. A
pulse oximeter reading below about 92 percent suggests low oxygen and should be taken seriously.

## When to seek emergency care
Call 911 if you cannot catch your breath, cannot speak in full sentences, or are struggling to
breathe. Treat breathing trouble as an emergency when it comes with chest pain, blue or gray lips
or face, confusion, or a feeling that you cannot get air. Sudden, severe shortness of breath
always needs urgent medical attention.
