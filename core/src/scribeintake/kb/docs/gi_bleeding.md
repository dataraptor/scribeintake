# Gastrointestinal (GI) Bleeding

## What GI bleeding is
Gastrointestinal bleeding is not a disease but a symptom of a problem in the digestive tract.
Bleeding can come from anywhere along the tract, from the esophagus and stomach to the intestines
and rectum. The amount of bleeding can range from so small that only a lab test can find it to
large and obvious. Common causes include peptic ulcers, tears or inflammation in the esophagus,
hemorrhoids, and diverticular disease.

## Symptoms and when to seek emergency care
Signs of bleeding from the upper digestive tract include vomiting bright red blood or material
that looks like coffee grounds, and passing black or tarry stools. Bleeding from the lower tract
can cause bright red blood in or on the stool. Vomiting blood or passing black, tarry, or bloody
stools is a medical emergency — call 911 or go to the emergency room right away. Seek emergency
care if heavy bleeding comes with dizziness, weakness, fainting, a fast heartbeat, or abdominal
pain, because these can be signs of serious blood loss.
