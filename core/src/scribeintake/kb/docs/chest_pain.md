# Chest Pain: Causes and When It Is an Emergency

## Many causes, some serious
Chest pain has many possible causes, and some of them are life-threatening. Pain can come from the
heart, but also from the lungs, the esophagus, the muscles, the ribs, or the nerves of the chest
wall. Because it can be hard to tell the cause from the symptoms alone, new or unexplained chest
pain should be taken seriously. Heart-related chest pain is often felt as pressure, tightness,
squeezing, or aching, and it may spread to the shoulders, arms, neck, jaw, or back.

## When to seek emergency care
Call 911 if you have sudden crushing or squeezing chest pain, or chest pain that spreads to the
arm, jaw, neck, or back. Treat chest pain as an emergency when it comes with shortness of breath,
sweating, nausea, lightheadedness, or a feeling of impending doom. People with chest pain that is
not clearly from another cause should be evaluated promptly. Chest pain that stays only in the
chest with no spreading, sweating, or trouble breathing still deserves a same-day or prompt
clinical evaluation, because the cause cannot be confirmed without an exam and tests.
