# Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)

## What DKA is
Diabetic ketoacidosis (DKA) is a serious complication of diabetes that can be life-threatening. It
develops when the body does not have enough insulin and starts breaking down fat for fuel, which
produces acids called ketones that build up in the blood. DKA is most common in people with type 1
diabetes but can also happen in type 2. It is often triggered by illness, infection, or missed
insulin doses, and it usually happens when blood glucose is very high, often above 250 to 300
mg/dL.

## Symptoms and when to seek emergency care
Early symptoms include very high blood glucose, frequent urination, extreme thirst, and tiredness.
As DKA worsens, symptoms include nausea and vomiting, abdominal pain, fast deep breathing, a
fruity smell on the breath, confusion, and trouble staying awake. DKA is a medical emergency. Seek
emergency care or call 911 if you have high blood glucose with vomiting, abdominal pain, trouble
breathing, or confusion, or if you cannot keep fluids down. Checking for ketones when blood glucose
is high and acting early can prevent DKA from becoming dangerous.
