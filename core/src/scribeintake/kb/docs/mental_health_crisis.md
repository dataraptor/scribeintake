# Mental Health Crisis and the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline

## If you are in crisis, help is available now
If you or someone you know is struggling or in crisis, help is available. The 988 Suicide and
Crisis Lifeline connects you to trained crisis counselors. It is for anyone facing mental health
struggles, emotional distress, thoughts of suicide or self-harm, alcohol or drug use concerns, or
who just needs someone to talk to. The Lifeline is free and confidential, and it is available 24
hours a day, 7 days a week, 365 days a year.

## How to reach the Lifeline
You can reach the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline by calling or texting 988, or by chatting online
through the Lifeline website. Skilled, judgment-free counselors provide compassionate support and
can connect you to local resources. If you or someone else is in immediate danger of suicide or
self-harm, or has taken steps to act on those thoughts, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency
room. Thoughts of suicide, self-harm, or not wanting to be alive are a medical emergency and
deserve immediate help — you do not have to face them alone.
