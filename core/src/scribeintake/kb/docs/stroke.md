# Stroke: Recognize the Signs and Act F.A.S.T.

## Signs and symptoms of stroke
During a stroke, every minute counts, and fast treatment can lessen the brain damage that a
stroke can cause. The warning signs come on suddenly. They include sudden numbness or weakness in
the face, arm, or leg, especially on one side of the body; sudden confusion or trouble speaking or
understanding speech; sudden trouble seeing in one or both eyes; sudden trouble walking,
dizziness, or loss of balance and coordination; and a sudden severe headache with no known cause.

## Use the F.A.S.T. test
F.A.S.T. is an easy way to remember the most common signs of a stroke and what to do. F is for
face drooping: ask the person to smile and check whether one side of the face droops. A is for arm
weakness: ask the person to raise both arms and watch for one arm drifting downward. S is for
speech difficulty: ask the person to repeat a simple sentence and listen for slurred or strange
speech. T is for time to call 911: if you see any of these signs, call 911 right away and note the
time the symptoms first appeared.

## When to seek emergency care
Call 9-1-1 immediately if you or someone else has any stroke symptoms, even if the symptoms go
away. Note the time the symptoms started, because some stroke treatments work best only when given
within a few hours of the first symptom. Do not drive yourself to the hospital; let emergency
responders begin care on the way.

## Mini-strokes and warning signs
A transient ischemic attack (TIA), sometimes called a mini-stroke, causes the same symptoms as a
stroke but the symptoms last only a short time and then go away. A TIA is a warning sign that a
larger stroke may follow, and it is a medical emergency even after the symptoms stop. Do not
ignore stroke symptoms just because they get better. Call 911 and get evaluated right away.
Controlling high blood pressure, not smoking, and managing diabetes and cholesterol lower the risk
of stroke over time, but any sudden stroke symptom is always an emergency in the moment.
