# Vaginal Bleeding During Pregnancy

## What it can mean
Vaginal bleeding during pregnancy has many causes. Some light bleeding or spotting can be normal,
especially early in pregnancy, but bleeding can also be a sign of a serious problem such as
miscarriage, ectopic pregnancy, or, later in pregnancy, a problem with the placenta. Because the
cause cannot be known without an exam, any bleeding during pregnancy should be discussed with your
health care provider promptly.

## When to seek emergency care
Get medical care right away if you are pregnant and have heavy vaginal bleeding, bleeding with
severe abdominal or pelvic pain, bleeding with cramping, dizziness or fainting, or passing tissue.
Call your provider or go to the emergency room for these symptoms. Severe abdominal pain during
pregnancy, with or without bleeding, can be an emergency such as an ectopic pregnancy and needs
immediate evaluation. When bleeding is heavy or comes with severe pain, faintness, or signs of
shock, call 911.
